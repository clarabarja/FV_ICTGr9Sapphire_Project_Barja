Fire Accidents: Causes and Prevention


Common Causes of Fire Accidents




Cooking


Boiling liquids, grease and scorching oven temperatures make it easy for accidental fires to begin here..



Electrical Issues


Faulty wiring, power surges and electrical shorts can lead to smoke and ash.




Smoking


Smoke from cigarettes can easily start fires, like when they aren't disposed of properly and continue burning.

Prevention of Fire Accidents




Never Leave Cooking Unattended


Turn off ovens and stoves when finished cooking, and keep flammable items (tea towels, oven gloves) away from the cooker. Make sure to keep oven and grill pans free from oil build-up.



Always Attend to your Electrical Appliances


Never overload sockets and never leave electrical appliances (dishwashers, washing machines) running overnight or when leaving the house.



Be Responsible when Smoking


Never smoke in bed when feeling drowsy or tired, and make sure to always stub cigarettes out fully in an ashtray.
